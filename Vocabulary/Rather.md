Is an adverb, and it has a few common meanings depending on the context - but they all relate to preference, degree or contrast

1. To show preference - Would rather + verb
	Used when you prefer one thing over another
	Examples: *"I'd(i would) rather drink tea than coffe"* - I prefer tea.

2. To show degree or intestify - Meaning quite, fairly or a bit
	Used to soften or strengthen and adjective/adverb
	Examples: *"It's rather cold today"* - It's a bit/quite cold

3. To correct or contrast something - Meaning more exactly or instead
	Examples: "She's not lazy; rather, she's tired"
